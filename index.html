Is it possible to surf in Seattle?

Yes, it is totally possible to surf in Seattle! There are two ways: scoring wind waves, and surfing ferry or freighter ship waves. Both require a little luck and a decent amount of planning.

How to surf wind waves

Wind is responsible for creating almost all waves on Earth1. In the ocean, huge storms spanning hundreds of miles can generate giant swells, but here in Puget Sound we only have a few miles of open water to work with. As a result, we need sustained high winds in order to build enough wind chop to create surfable waves.

You'll need:

  • Using an app like windy.com, keep an eye on the wind forecast. Any sustained winds above 25kt for 2 or more hours are likely to create surfable conditions in the Puget Sound.

    To monitor Puget Sound winds for the Seattle area in almost real-time, take a look at the West Point (WPOW1) weather station at Discovery Park.

  • Using the wind direction as your guide, choose a spot that offers the longest draw of wind across water. When the winds are out of the south-southwest, Alki Point is the place to be. When winds are from the North, Lowman Beach can be a better bet.

  • Wind storms are more frequent in the winter months, but so are extremely high tides. For surfable waves, a tide between 5 - 10 is preferable. Follow Seattle tides using NOAA's Tides and Currents website.

  • Even with an ideal forecast, things still may not come together. Conditions change quickly, so it may pay to watch and wait before packing it in!

How to surf ferry and freighter waves

Ferries and large boats like container ships or tankers can create surfable waves, but the size of the boat is less important than the speed the boat is travelling. The many "fast ferry" boats that traverse Puget Sound are significantly smaller than container ships, yet fast ferry waves mimic ocean swell better than any other boats in Puget Sound.

You'll need:

  • What would it be like to predict within a couple of minutes when a set wave would come in? With boat-tracking apps, that is completely normal! Real-time vessel telemetry is available from apps like marinetraffic.com, so you can time your session with to coincide with the waves you want to catch!

  • The premiere location for tanker/container ship surfing is Shilshole Bay in Ballard. The best spot for ferry wave surfing is Duwamish head in West Seattle.

  • These waves only break when the tide is very low. Look for a 4 or under on NOAA's Tides and Currents website.
